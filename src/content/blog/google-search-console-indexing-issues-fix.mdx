Indexing statuses in Google Search Console are not moral judgments—they are **machine-readable explanations** of where a URL sits in Google’s crawl/index pipeline. Your job is to translate the status into **an engineerable fix**, then revalidate.

---

## What Indexing Issues Mean

When a URL is not indexed—or indexed differently than you expect—something in **discovery, crawl, rendering, quality evaluation, or canonicalization** disagrees with your assumptions. Start with the *Indexing* reports and drill into **example URLs** for patterns.

---

## Common GSC Indexing Errors

You will repeatedly see:

- Excluded by `noindex`
- Alternate page with proper canonical tag
- Crawled – currently not indexed
- Discovered – currently not indexed
- Duplicate without user-selected canonical
- Soft 404
- Page with redirect
- Server errors (5xx) and fetch anomalies

Treat counts as **prioritization**, not panic—sample URLs matter more than totals.

---

## Crawled — Currently Not Indexed

Google **fetched** the URL but chose **not** to index it. Common drivers include **perceived duplication**, **thin or boilerplate value**, **low differential utility vs other URLs**, or **site-wide quality signals**.

**Fixes (pattern-dependent):**

- Differentiate content where multiple URLs answer the same intent.
- Improve depth, E‑E‑A‑T signals, and internal links from authoritative hubs.
- Consolidate near-duplicates and canonicalize thoughtfully.
- Address thin parameter or facet URLs at template level.

---

## Discovered — Currently Not Indexed

Google knows the URL exists but **has not crawled** it yet—or deprioritized crawl. Check **internal links**, **sitemap hygiene**, **crawl depth**, and whether robots/noindex/navigation accidentally strands URLs.

---

## Canonicalization Problems

If Google selects an unexpected canonical, verify:

- **Consistent** canonical tags and internal link destinations.
- **HTTPS** preference and hostname consistency (`www` vs apex).
- **Parameter handling** and faceted navigation rules.
- Hreflang and regional variants **not fighting** primary canonicals.

---

## Noindex Mistakes

Staging tokens, CMS checkboxes, “discourage search engines” settings, and **accidental robots meta** on templates are classic slip-ups. Diff templates after releases; compare HTML source, not just the visual page.

---

## Sitemap Issues

Sitemaps should list **canonical, 200-status URLs** you want discovered—not every parameterized evergreen. Watch for:

- Massive stale URLs
- 404/410 chains
- Non-canonical locations
- Segmented sitemaps without clear ownership

---

## Internal Linking Fixes

Weak pages often lack **paths from pages Google already trusts**. Add **contextual** links from relevant parents; avoid sitewide footer dumps as your only strategy.

---

## Technical SEO Troubleshooting

Bring in server logs or CDN analytics **only after** URL-level patterns emerge—otherwise you drown in noise. Standard sequence:

1. Identify cohort (template, directory, brand vs non-brand).
2. Confirm HTTP/HTML reality (status, redirects, canonical, robots).
3. Patch template or policy—**not just one URL**.
4. Request validation / retest in GSC where available.
5. Monitor the same cohort week over week.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### How long until indexing recovers after a fix?

It varies by site authority, crawl demand, and scope. Small fixes can improve in days; **template-level** issues may take multiple recrawl cycles.

### Should I “Request indexing” for every URL?

No—use it for **strategic** URLs or after meaningful fixes. Bulk requests do not shortcut quality evaluation.

### Does “indexed” guarantee traffic?

No. Indexing is **eligibility** to rank; relevance, competition, SERP layout, and intent fit still govern clicks.
